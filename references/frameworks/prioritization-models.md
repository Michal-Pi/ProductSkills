# Prioritization Models

Choose the model based on the decision being made. Do not default to RICE.

## Model Selection

| Situation | Use | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Compare feature bets with known reach | RICE | Balances reach, impact, confidence, and effort. |
| Sequence time-sensitive work | WSJF or cost of delay | Makes delay cost explicit. |
| Rank experiments quickly | ICE | Lightweight for uncertain bets. |
| Negotiate release scope | MoSCoW | Separates must-have from optional scope. |
| Rank discovered customer opportunities | Opportunity scoring | Weighs importance and satisfaction gaps. |
| Compare strategic tradeoffs | Weighted scorecard | Lets the team encode context-specific criteria. |
| Assess delight and table stakes | Kano | Distinguishes basic needs from delighters. |
| Optimize economic sequencing | CD3 | Compares cost of delay against duration. |

## Required Output

Every prioritization output should include:

- Options considered
- Criteria and weights
- Score or ordering
- Evidence confidence
- Sensitivity or tie-break notes
- Recommendation
- Risks and next action

## Guardrails

- Do not rank work without naming assumptions and confidence.
- Do not mix incomparable work unless the decision context requires it.
- If the model is a poor fit, say so and choose a simpler decision rule.
