# Growth Models

Use growth modeling to explain how the product acquires, activates, retains, monetizes, and expands users.

## Core Structures

- Funnel: acquisition -> activation -> retention -> monetization -> referral or expansion.
- Loop: an action by one user creates value or distribution that brings back or attracts users.
- Lifecycle: new, activated, retained, at-risk, resurrected, expanded.
- Product-led growth: product usage creates sales, expansion, or advocacy signal.

## Diagnosis Steps

1. Name the business objective and target segment.
2. Map the current growth model.
3. Identify the constrained stage or loop.
4. Separate symptoms from root causes.
5. Form hypotheses.
6. Design experiments with guardrails.
7. Define decision thresholds.

## Required Output

Growth work should include target metric, baseline if available, segment, bottleneck, hypothesis, proposed experiment, success metric, guardrail metric, and decision rule.

## Guardrails

- Do not optimize acquisition if activation or retention is the binding constraint.
- Do not treat a funnel drop as a root cause without evidence.
- Do not propose experiments without a measurable decision threshold.
