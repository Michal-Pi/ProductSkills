# Continuous Discovery

Use continuous discovery when product decisions need repeated customer learning instead of one-off research.

## Core Ideas

- Keep a weekly customer touchpoint cadence when possible.
- Represent the decision space as outcome -> opportunities -> solutions -> assumptions.
- Separate customer evidence from product interpretation.
- Prioritize assumptions by risk, evidence strength, and decision impact.
- Convert weak evidence into the next smallest research or experiment step.

## Opportunity Solution Tree

Use this structure:

1. Desired outcome
2. Customer opportunities
3. Solution ideas
4. Assumptions
5. Tests

## Evidence Quality

- High: direct recent customer behavior, repeated interview patterns, usage data, or paid commitment.
- Medium: consistent qualitative signal, sales/support patterns, prototype test evidence, or limited cohort data.
- Low: opinions, single anecdotes, internal belief, competitor mimicry, or unverified stakeholder requests.

## Guardrails

- Do not turn a solution idea into a delivery commitment before naming the riskiest assumptions.
- Do not hide uncertainty behind polished PRD language.
- When evidence is missing, recommend the next learning step.
