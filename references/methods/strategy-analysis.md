# Strategy Analysis

Use strategy analysis when choosing product direction, market focus, positioning, pricing, or sequencing.

## Strategy Inputs

- Customer segment and job
- Market or category dynamics
- Alternatives and switching costs
- Product differentiation
- Distribution constraints
- Business model and pricing
- Company strategy and constraints
- Evidence confidence

## Common Methods

| Need | Method |
|---|---|
| Understand market context | Market map or category analysis |
| Choose target customer | Segmentation and ICP analysis |
| Compare alternatives | Competitive evaluation |
| Estimate upside | Opportunity sizing |
| Decide what to do first | Prioritization model selection |
| Align goals | OKRs and strategy narrative |
| Price or package | Value metric and willingness-to-pay analysis |
| Message a change | Positioning and proof-point analysis |

## Output Standard

Strategy outputs should include options, evidence, assumptions, constraints, tradeoffs, recommendation, confidence, and the next decision or learning step.

## Guardrails

- Do not confuse competitor parity with customer value.
- Do not present a single-point market estimate without a confidence range.
- Do not use prioritization scores as a substitute for strategy.
