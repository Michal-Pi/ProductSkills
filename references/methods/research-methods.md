# Research Methods

Use this reference when choosing a discovery or validation method.

## Method Selection

| Question | Method | Best For | Watch For |
|---|---|---|---|
| What problem is the customer trying to solve? | Customer interview | Jobs, pains, workarounds, buying triggers | Leading questions and overgeneralizing from one user |
| What patterns exist across feedback? | VoC synthesis | Support, sales, surveys, reviews, interview notes | Mixing source quality and volume |
| How do users currently complete the job? | Contextual inquiry | Workflow reality and hidden constraints | Small sample size |
| Can users complete this design? | Usability test | Comprehension, flow, friction, errors | Testing preference instead of behavior |
| How common is the problem? | Survey | Frequency, segmentation, ranking | Poor sampling and vague wording |
| What does behavior show? | Product analytics | Funnel, cohort, retention, feature usage | Instrumentation gaps and survivorship bias |
| What outcome matters? | Jobs-to-be-done analysis | Progress sought, alternatives, triggers | Treating jobs as personas |

## Jobs-To-Be-Done Notes

Capture:

- Situation or trigger
- Desired progress
- Current alternative
- Functional, social, and emotional dimensions
- Success criteria
- Switching forces

## Research Plan Standard

Every research plan should name the decision, method, audience, screener, questions or tasks, synthesis approach, confidence risk, and decision rule.
