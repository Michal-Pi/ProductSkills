# Sample PRD: Evidence-Linked Planning Workspace

## Objective

Help mid-market Product Ops and PM teams turn messy evidence into traceable PRDs and safe delivery previews.

## Customer

Product Ops leads and PMs at B2B SaaS companies with 5 to 25 PMs using Notion and Linear.

## Evidence

- INT-001: evidence context dies during PRD handoff.
- INT-003 and SALES-002: strong demand for preview-only Linear/Notion sync.
- SUP-001, SUP-003, SUP-008, SUP-009: evidence traceability and exportability issues.
- Usage analytics: Product Ops segment has 78 percent activation and 72 percent week 4 retention.

## Problem

Teams lack a reliable path from evidence to prioritized opportunities, PRDs, and delivery artifacts. Trust drops when generated docs cannot cite sources or when external tool actions feel unsafe.

## Assumptions

- Evidence-linked PRDs will reduce review friction.
- Dry-run previews create value before live sync exists.
- Mid-market and Product Ops teams are a stronger fit than SMB founders.

## Scope

- Evidence-linked opportunity synthesis.
- PRD review and generation with cited evidence.
- Linear and Notion dry-run preview artifacts.
- Missing-evidence and confidence labels.

## Non-Goals

- No live external writes.
- No GitHub Issues support.
- No raw recording ingestion.
- No security certification claims.

## Success Metrics

- 30 percent reduction in PRD preparation time for pilot users.
- 80 percent of generated PRDs include cited evidence for problem, scope, and metrics.
- Zero unintended external writes.
- Product Ops week 4 retention remains above 70 percent.

## Risks

- Users may over-trust generated PRDs.
- Missing enterprise security evidence may block larger deals.
- Poor support-ticket import quality may weaken synthesis.

## Open Questions

- What minimum evidence threshold should be required before PRD generation?
- Which preview payload fields are required for future confirmed writes?
- What pricing is viable for Product Ops teams?
