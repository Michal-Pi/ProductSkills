# Positioning and Messaging

Use when defining how a product change should be explained to the market or internal teams.

## Steps

1. Identify audience, problem, alternative, and value.
2. State positioning: for whom, who need, this is, that provides, unlike.
3. Draft primary message and proof points.
4. Tailor by channel or stakeholder group.
5. Identify claims that need evidence or legal review.
6. Define next review owner.

## Output

Return positioning, message, proof, channel variants, risks, and open questions.

## Done when

- The positioning statement (for whom, who need, this is, that provides, unlike) is fully populated; audience, problem, alternative, and value are each named, not implied.
- At least two audience-segmented messages are produced (e.g., end user, buyer, internal field, existing customer), each with proof points cited to evidence (data, customer quote, benchmark) or explicitly marked "claim - needs evidence."
- Claims requiring legal, compliance, or factual review are flagged separately, and the next review owner is named.
- When audience, problem, or differentiation cannot be stated from supplied inputs, the procedure refuses to publish messaging and returns those gaps as the next research step.
