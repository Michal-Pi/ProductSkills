# Post-Launch Review

Use after launch to evaluate adoption, impact, and learning.

## Steps

1. Compare actual metrics against launch goals.
2. Review adoption by segment and channel.
3. Summarize customer feedback, support burden, sales impact, and product issues.
4. Separate expected misses from surprises.
5. Recommend continue, iterate, expand, roll back manually, or retire.
6. Capture learnings for future launches.

## Output

Return results, evidence, lessons, decisions, and next actions.

## Done when

- Each launch goal metric is compared against actuals with source cited (analytics query, dashboard, survey, support volume); metrics without data are explicitly marked "no data" rather than estimated.
- Adoption is broken down by segment and channel, and feedback from customers, support, and sales is summarized with at least one quote or ticket reference per signal.
- Expected misses are held separate from surprises; a single recommendation (continue, iterate, expand, roll back, or retire) is recorded with the rule that produced it.
- At least three lessons are captured for future launches with concrete actions (process change, gate addition, doc update), not generic platitudes; when launch goals were never set, the review refuses to grade outcomes and instead documents the goal-setting gap.
