# OKR Setting

Use when translating product strategy into objectives and measurable key results.

## Steps

1. Clarify company or product outcome.
2. Identify customer and business constraints.
3. Draft one focused objective.
4. Create measurable key results with baseline, target, owner, and time horizon.
5. Identify initiatives separately from key results.
6. Check for metric gaming and guardrails.

## Output

Return objective, key results, rationale, guardrails, initiatives, and review cadence.

## Done when

- Exactly one focused objective is stated; the company or product outcome it ladders to is cited.
- Each key result has a measurable metric, baseline, target, owner, and time horizon; baselines that cannot be sourced from supplied data are labeled "baseline unknown" rather than guessed.
- Initiatives are listed separately from key results, and at least one guardrail metric is attached to address the most likely metric-gaming path; the rationale names the gaming risk it counters.
- When the upstream strategy or outcome is undefined, the procedure refuses to commit targets and returns a strategy-clarification question list instead.
