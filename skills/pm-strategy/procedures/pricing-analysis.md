# Pricing Analysis

Use when evaluating packaging, price changes, monetization, willingness to pay, or plan design.

## Steps

1. Identify buyer, user, budget holder, and value metric.
2. Map current packaging and constraints.
3. Compare alternatives, competitor pricing, willingness-to-pay evidence, and usage value.
4. Identify monetization assumptions and risks.
5. Recommend tests such as interviews, fake-door pricing, cohort analysis, or sales validation.
6. Define decision criteria and guardrails.

## Output

Return pricing options, evidence, assumptions, risks, validation plan, and recommendation.

## Done when

- Buyer, user, budget holder, and value metric are each named distinctly; current packaging and constraints (contract terms, discounting practice, plan boundaries) are mapped.
- At least three pricing options or alternatives are compared with cited evidence for competitor pricing, willingness-to-pay signal, or usage value; cells without a source are tagged "assumption."
- Monetization risks (downgrade, churn, sales-cycle friction, channel conflict) are listed with guardrails, and the validation plan names at least one bounded test (interview, fake-door, cohort analysis, sales test) with a decision rule.
- When willingness-to-pay evidence is absent and no test is available before the decision date, the procedure refuses to recommend a price change and routes to a validation plan instead.
