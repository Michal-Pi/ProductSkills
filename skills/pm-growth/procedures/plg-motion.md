# Product-Led Growth Motion

Use when diagnosing product-led growth, sales-assist handoffs, or expansion motions.

## Steps

1. Identify user, buyer, champion, and admin.
2. Map self-serve path, activation, collaboration, conversion, expansion, and sales-assist handoff.
3. Identify product-qualified signals.
4. Recommend PLG experiments and enablement needs.

## Output

Return PLG motion map, qualified signals, bottlenecks, handoff risks, experiment ideas, guardrails, and next action.

## Done when

- User, buyer, champion, and admin are named distinctly; the motion map covers self-serve, activation, collaboration, conversion, expansion, and sales-assist handoff (or marks each stage "not present" with a reason).
- At least three named product-qualified signals are listed, each with a definition or threshold (event, frequency, or account-level rule) tied to supplied data when available.
- At least three PLG experiments are recommended, each with a target metric, a guardrail (revenue, support load, deliverability, or brand), and a decision rule; sales-assist handoff risks have an owner.
- When the supplied context cannot distinguish user from buyer or has no usage signal to ground PQL definitions, the procedure flags the gap and refuses to commit a PQL threshold.
