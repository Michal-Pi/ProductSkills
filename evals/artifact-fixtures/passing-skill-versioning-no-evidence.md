# Decision

Do not draft committed PRD scope for the skill versioning and management product yet. The correct status is needs validation because customer evidence is missing.

# Current Evidence

Facts: this is currently a founder hypothesis about startups needing skill versioning, rollout, governance, and management. Missing evidence: no customer interviews, no usage data, no sales notes, no support tickets, and no pricing signal.

# Assumptions

This separates assumptions from facts. Assumptions: startups have repeated pain managing skill versions, multiple teammates need governance, current workarounds are risky, and buyers will pay before the problem becomes enterprise-scale.

# Riskiest Assumption

The riskiest assumption is desirability: startup teams feel enough acute pain from skill versioning and management to adopt a dedicated product now. Confidence is low because there is no direct customer evidence.

# Research Method

Use a research plan before PRD. Interview 8 to 10 startup founders, product leads, or AI operations owners who currently use reusable prompts, agents, or skills. Screen for teams with multiple people editing or deploying reusable AI workflows.

# Audience

Target early-stage startups with at least three reusable AI workflows, more than one contributor editing skills or prompts, and some concern about rollout, quality, or version drift.

# Success Metric

At least 6 of 10 qualified participants describe a recent, specific, costly problem with versioning, reviewing, rolling out, or reverting skills.

# Guardrail Metric

Do not count curiosity or generic interest as validation. Guard against false demand by requiring a recent workaround, incident, or budget owner.

# Decision Rule

Proceed to PRD only if repeated pain, current workaround cost, and buyer/user ownership are validated. Otherwise revise the opportunity or continue discovery.

# Next Action

Names concrete next actions: recruit target participants, run the interview script, synthesize direct evidence versus inference, and ask for an approval gate before requirements are drafted.
