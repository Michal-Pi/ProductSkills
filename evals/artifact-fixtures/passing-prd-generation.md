# Objective

Create an evidence-backed problem statement for onboarding teams that are losing qualified trial users before setup completion.

# Customer

Primary users are operations leads at small B2B teams who invited teammates but did not finish workspace setup.

# Evidence

This artifact includes direct evidence and states where evidence is missing. Direct evidence: four support tickets mention unclear setup ownership, two interviews mention teammate invite confusion, and analytics show a 31 percent drop between invite sent and first teammate action. Evidence strength is medium because the interview sample is small.

# Assumptions

This section separates assumptions from facts. Assumption: clearer owner assignment will increase first teammate action. Fact: the invite-to-action step has the largest measured setup drop.

# Scope

In scope: owner assignment, teammate invite status, and first-action reminders. Out of scope: billing changes and admin permission redesign.

# Non Goals

Explicit non-goals: do not rebuild all onboarding, do not change pricing, and do not create a generalized notification center.

# Success Metrics

Measurable success metrics: increase first teammate action from 42 percent to 55 percent within 30 days, keep setup support tickets below current baseline, and maintain activation quality.

# Risks

Includes confidence or risk notes. Risk: reminders may annoy invited teammates. Confidence is medium because the direct customer evidence is directional but not yet validated by an experiment.

# Open Questions

Which teammate role should receive the first reminder, and does owner assignment help self-serve accounts without sales assistance?

# Next Actions

Names concrete next actions: run a prototype test with five admins, define event tracking for teammate first action, and review the experiment result before committing delivery scope.
